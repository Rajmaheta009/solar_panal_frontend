    WHAT ARE THE TYPES OF SOLAR PANELS?

    There are three types of Solar Panels accessible with us such as monocrystalline, polycrystalline, and thin-film panels. Each type of panels has its own unique benefits and the solar panel type best suited for your installation will depend on factors specific to your own property and desired system characteristics.

    Monocrystalline Solar Panel

    The solar panel which is made of monocrystalline silicon is the purest one and easily recognized by their unvarying dark look and the curved ends. The offered solar panel has a high power output, occupy less space, and last the longest. Another advantage to consider is that they tend to be slightly less affected by high temperatures compared to polycrystalline panels.

    Polycrystalline Solar Panel

    This type of solar panel can be quickly indentified due to their square shape, its angles are not cut and it has a blue, speckled look. The offered Polycrystalline Solar Panel is manufactured by melting raw silicon, which is a faster and cheaper process. However, the differences between mono- and polycrystalline types of solar panels are not so significant and the choice will strongly depend on your specific situation. The first option offers slightly higher space efficiency at a slightly higher price but power outputs is basically the same.

    Thin-film panels

    Thin film solar panels are made of the thin film solar cell materials. These devices are made from physically flexible solar cells and are designed to generate electrical energy by converting light energy into electricity. There are several types of thin film solar panels available today made with various technologies. A major advantage of thin film solar lies in its flexibility, which helps it being installed on curved as well as flat surfaces and in building-integrated photovoltaics.
